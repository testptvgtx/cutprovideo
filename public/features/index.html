Product capabilities

A local-first AI video editor that runs in your browser

Timeline Studio combines AI voice generation, automatic captions, talking portraits, visual editing and a multi-track timeline in one installable web app. Core editing and inference workflows run on the user's device; model files are downloaded on demand and cached for repeat use.

AI creation

Multilingual AI voiceovers

Chinese voices use Piper/VITS browser ONNX. English voices use Kokoro 82M ONNX. Browser Piper voices are also available for German, Spanish, French, Italian and Brazilian Portuguese.

Automatic captions

Whisper small q8 runs in a WASM worker. Caption timing is adjusted toward nearby audio energy, with conservative cleanup for high-confidence Chinese homophone errors.

Talking portraits

JoyVASA drives facial motion from audio and LivePortrait renders the result with a WebGPU path, including 256px preview and 512px quality modes.

Browser vision

YOLOS tiny detects subjects and MODNet creates portrait masks for smart cropping, caption avoidance and background removal in preview and export.

Timeline and visual editing

Multi-track timeline

Arrange visuals, stickers, captions, source audio, generated voiceovers and music. Split, duplicate, move, resize, hide, lock and delete selectable clips.

Visual keyframes

Animate scale, horizontal and vertical position, rotation and opacity with grouped or property-level keyframes and independent linear interpolation.

Masks and effects

Use rectangle, rounded-rectangle and circular masks with canvas-accurate position, size, radius, feathering and inversion, plus filters and transitions.

Video speed and audio sync

Adjust clip-scoped playback speed and choose whether extracted source audio remains linked to the visual clip or can diverge independently.

Export and privacy

Video outputMP4 when the browser supports the required recording or conversion path; WebM is the fallback.
ResolutionExport controls include 720p, 1080p, 1440p and 2160p choices, subject to device capability.
Frame rate30fps and 60fps options are available, subject to browser and device performance.
Data modelMedia is selected from the user's device and processed in the browser. Third-party model hosting may receive normal network requests when model assets are downloaded.
